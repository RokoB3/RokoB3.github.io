Main Human Vulnerabilities

An introduction to social engineering.

Definition

Social engineering is an attack vector that relies heavily on human interaction and often involves manipulating people into breaking normal security procedures.

Phishing attacks

A type of social engineering attack mostly used to capture or steal user data, such as login credentials and credit card numbers. It occurs when an attacker, masquerading as a trusted entity, dupes a victim into opening an email, instant message or text message. The victim then opens a malicious link that may lead to the installation of malware, the freezing of their system as part of a ransomware attack, or the theft of sensitive information.

Bad password habits

To keep your passwords safe, it's crucial to make the right choices when creating them. All passwords should be unique, containing at least one upper and lower case character, a number and symbol.

Has your password been compromised?

The independent website Have I Been Pwned is an effective way to track issues with data breaches, and it allows for searches by both email address and password to see if you have fallen victim to a credential attack. Additionally, popular tools such as 1Password and LastPass allow users to automatically generate new passwords for new accounts while not having to go to the trouble of remembering them.

Online Anonymity

The internet is filled with big companies who, with them help of ISPs (Internet Service Provider), are stealing your information. But how can you stop them?

Private Browser Mode

When private browsing is activated, the browser stop collecting information (cookies, history, form data). Some processes might still run in the background but they are discarded at the end of each session.

VPNs (Virtual private networks)

A VPN allows you to browse privately and securely, encrypting your data and hiding your location. But not all VPNs are built the same. You need, for example, to be wary when a service is free and of course a VPN that logs your data is a definite no.

The Tor Browser

The Tor browser is the greatest web browser that anonymizes your web traffic using its own Tor network, making it easy to protect your identity online. Its mainly used to access a very special part of the internet, unreachable by any other browser, widely recognized as the Dark Web. Although its name makes it seem very suspicious and scary, the Dark Web is nothing more than a .onion at the end of a domain name instead of .com or .ca. In fact, many known websites have Tor webpages such as The New York Times (https://www.nytimes3xbfgragh.onion/) and even Facebook (https://www.facebookcorewwwi.onion)! Note: These websites are only be visible when visited with the Tor browser.

Protecting yourself against malware

Virus Eradication.

Attack types

There are many different types of attacks including Viruses, Worms, Trojans, Spyware, Keyloggers, Ransomware, Botnets, Logic Bombs, network attacks such as DDOS and Man-In-The-Middle, and social engineering attacks like phishing that are capable of harming your computer.

Antivirus software

Trusted antiviruses are very effective in protecting your computer against a large set of malwares including: Viruses, rootkits, bots, worms, trojan horses, spyware, and dangerous emails.

Dangerous USB

BadUSB revolves around the fact that many different devices plug into the same USB connectors. By hacking the code of the USB micro-controller of an "innocent" device, like a USB memory stick, you can turn it into something far more capable, such as a keyboard or a network card. Stick the device into a computer and it could execute commands or even a malicious program without the owner knowing.
